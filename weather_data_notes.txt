About ERA5 spread variables:
ERA5 - the reanalysis model, underpins Open-Meteo historical/archive API, data available from 1940.
Do not need spread variables - not doing weather forecasting.
ERA5 spread is model uncertainty not weather conditions.
ERA5 - Ensemble model of 10 sims, spread is gap between the highest
and lowest of those 10 results, how much the models disagreed.

Selected features (Total = 9), see below
key params(6):
solar:
shortwave_radiation_wm2 — total incoming solar radiation, primary driver of solar panel output
cloud_cover_pct — blocks solar radiation, affects solar generation
direct_radiation_wm2 — more precise solar measure for direct beam radiation on panels
diffuse_radiation_wm2 — cloudy day solar — panels still generate from diffuse light

wind:
wind_speed_100m_ms — best for wind turbine output bc turbines operate at 80-150m not ground level
wind_gusts_10m_ms — extreme wind events can cause turbine shutdown, affects generation

temp and pressure:
temperature_2m_c — drives electricity demand, cold = heating = more gas needed
snowfall - as above (and literal sesonality), causes demand spikes bc cold/solar panel coverage/turbne icing = more gas
pressure_msl_hpa — indicates weather system type, low pressure = storms = high wind, high pressure = calm = low wind and high solar in summer but cold in winter


dropped params (9):
wind_speed_10m_ms — superseded by 100m for energy modelling
wind_direction_10m_deg and wind_direction_100m_deg — direction doesn't affect generation amount
cloud_cover_low_pct, cloud_cover_mid_pct, cloud_cover_high_pct — total cloud cover already captures this
direct_normal_irradiance_wm2, global_tilted_irradiance_wm2, terrestrial_radiation_wm2 — redundant given shortwave and direct radiation already included
